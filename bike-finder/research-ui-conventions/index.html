Research: UI Conventions

Shopping List:

  1. Dark grey cable-knit sweater;
  2. Riding lawn mower;
  3. Two-line high-performance kite;
  4. Mid-range gaming laptop.

Dark grey cable-knit sweater

  • thumbnail changes on hover, showing a different view of the model;
  • thumbnail changes when selecting radio buttons to change color;
  • same radio buttons available on the detailed page;
  • size options as radio buttons on the detailed page;
  • after adding to cart, a side panel of current view of the shopping cart moves in;
    • an X button will follow the cursor when hovering outside of the side panel;
    • while this visual representation is not conventional, it is intuitive that clicking outside of the panel will close the overlay.

Riding lawn mower

  • button to add to cart in thumbnail view;
  • checkboxes to compare multiple products;
  • star ratings clickable, jumping to review section;
  • select store to deliver from, which triggers an overlay to enter zipcode;
  • select shipping method from a list of radio buttons;
  • warranty available after adding to cart;
  • not as many personalized options compared to the sweater.

Two-line high-performance kite

  • before adding to cart, there are buttons where you can click to include accessories to purchase together with the kite;
  • descriptions and specifications collapsed into dropdowns;
  • radio buttons to select color;
  • color indicator showing whether the product is in stock or available to ship.

Mid-range gaming laptop.

  • thumbnail photo is not interactive as these laptops vary little on appearance;
  • shortcut button to shop directly in thumbnail view;
  • shows essential specs in thumbnail view, which takes up a lot of space;
    • as a result, each product takes up larger space than usual thumbnails;
  • many, many sections of radio buttons that allows customization;
  • checkboxes to include assets or accessories that comes with the laptop;
  • toggle betewen list view and card view;
  • option to save or print specs and customization info;
  • option to share specs and customization info on social media or via email.

Summary

Universal

  • When comparing products in thumbnail view, it is common to see one or two quick actions available below each thumbnail, such as radio buttons for color selection or CTA button for adding to cart;
  • In detailed view, all imgae galleries reside on the left side of the screen, while essential information and CTA button are on the right side.

Specific

  • While it is common to see an overlay of cart summary after adding the product to the cart, it is non-unanimous where the overlay resides on the screen;
  • When there is a high level of customization available to the product, there tends to be some navigation options to guide the user to the section they want;
  • Products that provides more functionality tends to emphasize more on customer reviews;
  • The site for the mower is the only one that confirms the user selection of warranty before adding to cart;
  • The site for the laptop is the only one that has the back to top button.

Reflection

  • While it is porbably intuitive that clicking outside of the overlay will close it, I think Almada Label does a good job calling that option out and can be applicable to all other sites;
  • It stood out to me that all of these websites have a clear sense of whether their products may be purchased in bulk. For those that are likely to be pruchased one at a time, the site eliminates the option to select quantity;
  • I like Cubcadet as it shows me estimated delivery days even before I add to cart;
  • Cyber Power PC's customization section annoys me a little because it promotes lots of unrelated accessories while marking them as "popular".
