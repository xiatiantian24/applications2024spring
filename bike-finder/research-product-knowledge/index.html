From research, I've learned that bike shopping websites are typically devided into multiple subpages based on the type of road it is used on, such as:

  • Mountain;
  • Road;
  • Gravel;
  • City.

Depending on the road type, these bikes might be designed for fater speed on paved roads, better stability on off-road trails and rugged terrains, better for urban commuting, more comfort for leisure rides, or for stunt riding and tricks, etc.


Alternatively, the website might also be divided based on the demographic of the audience and their purpose, such as:

  • Kids;
  • Students who are pramirily renting a bike instead of buying one;
  • Parts of a bike rather than an entire bike;
  • Electric.

Other specifications of a bike may include the material (steel for durability, aluminum for lighter weight and corrosion-resistant, carbon fiber for super light weight), the gear system (single or complex), the amount of uspension, the geometry of the frame, the break system (rim or disc), etc.


Summary

Purpose: I would personally use a bike for daily commute. It would be primarily used on city streets but may also need to handle situations like muddy or rugged roads.

Prioritization: I would prioritize safety, price, and brand reputation.

Features: A lightweight and durable material like aluminum or carbon fiber. Reliable and responsive brakes for safety. For the tire, I care about the stability on varied terrains and the durability.
